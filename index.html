The Science

Why Breathwork Works

You already know how to breathe. You've been doing it since the moment you were born. But somewhere along the way, most of us stopped doing it well. Stress, screens, busy lives and years of sitting hunched over desks have quietly trained us to breathe from the chest. And when we breath from the chest, shallow and fast, it tells the body to "stay alert", even if we want to sleep. Sound familiar?

The good news is that when we breath using a few simple techniques called "breathwork", you can control your body, your mind, and ultimately your health.

It is not surprising that some of these techniques have been around centuries for healing and wellbeing. Today, these techniques are being used to boost performance for elite athletes at the olympics, increase endurance for astronaughts in space, and magnify focus and concentration for executives in the business world.

But breathwork has an equally powerful benefit besides performance, and that's how it affects your body, your mind, and your wellbeing.

Think of your body having two modes: the first mode is shallow chest breathing that takes from your body for stress or survival, and the second is belly breathing that gives to your body for relaxation and healing. Unfortunately, most of us spend too much time in the first mode because of work, what's going on in the news, the pressures of learning and careers, and so on. You body may have 'forgotten' how to turn off this first mode, and just like a leaky tap, your body runs dry. Breathwork is your body's inbuilt intervention. You were born with this tool, free to use whenever you need.

And it works for everyone. A child with ADHD to calm their mind in a world that feels too fast. A new mother to 'reset' her displaced diaphragm after child birth. A student to focus during an exam. It all comes back to the breath.

Retreat Therapies

Cold Immersion & Infrared Sauna

Breathwork and thermal therapy are one of the most powerful combinations in human performance and recovery science. At the retreat, Mike integrates cold immersion and infrared sauna into the full experience.

Cold Immersion

Cold exposure paired with controlled breathing activates the vagus nerve, floods the body with norepinephrine, reduces inflammation, and builds a genuine tolerance for discomfort — on and off the field. The Wim Hof Method makes cold immersion something you actually enjoy.

Infrared Sauna

Deep heat penetrates tissue at a cellular level — relaxing muscle, improving circulation, and creating the conditions for profound physical recovery. After a breathwork session in the sauna, the body is open and receptive in a way that is difficult to achieve any other way.

Who It's For

Breathwork Works For Everyone

The research is clear and growing: intentional breathwork delivers measurable benefits across an extraordinary range of conditions, populations and life stages. If you have a nervous system — breathwork can help you.

Students & Exam Preparation

Breathwork slows the stress response and sharpens working memory. Students who practice before study sessions report better retention, less exam anxiety, and improved performance under pressure.

Athletes & Sport Performance

From the NFL to the Olympics, elite athletes use breathwork to optimise VO₂ max, accelerate recovery, and develop the mental edge that separates competitors at the highest level.

Gym & Fitness Performance

Proper breathing mechanics during training increases power output, reduces lactic acid build-up and dramatically shortens recovery time between sets — without changing anything else about your training.

Post-Pregnancy & Recovery

Diaphragmatic breathwork is one of the most effective tools for post-natal core rehabilitation, emotional regulation, and the nervous system reset that new mothers so urgently need.

Kids with ADD & ADHD

Deep breathing balances the autonomic nervous system, improving attention, reducing impulsivity and lowering hyperactivity — giving kids a drug-free tool they can use anywhere, anytime.

Also effective for: Surgery recovery · Elderly wellbeing · Public speaking anxiety · Chronic stress · Sleep disorders · Emotional regulation
